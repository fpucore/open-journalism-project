An incredibly important and alarming story came to light on Friday.

The letter is written by Senators Ron Wyden and Martin Heinrich and dated April 13, 2021.

[PUBLISH LETTER]

In the letter, Senators Wyden and Heinrich write to the Director of National Intelligence and the Director of the CIA.

They request a "declassification review" of the Privacy and Civil Liberties Oversight Board's review of "Executive Order 12333 Central Intelligence Agency Deep Dive II".

The letter goes on to state "The review covers the CIA's bulk [REDACTED]".

Now, Executive Order 12333 dates back to the early 1980s and was intended to extend the responsibilities of intelligence agencies and to direct the leaders of federal agencies to comply with CIA requests for information.

It has been amended since but those amendments have only worked to the favor of the CIA.

What CIA bulk collection program the letter refers to is still unclear as it remains secret and classified.

The Senators are highly critical of the secret program yet have not revealed any specific details about it.

Large chunks of the letter are heavily redacted which suggests the secret program being run by the CIA is extensive.

The point is made that lawmakers have worked hard to restrict, and in some cases prohibit, specific bulk data collection programs which which have previously been operated by the NSA.

It appears the CIA has expressed complete disregard to the changes made to data collection laws and oversight, instead running a bulk data collection program of its own in secret, which includes information on domestic American citizens.

Despite the CIA having claimed that it always operates within the scope of the law and is transparent with Congress, we know this is false because the Senators state disclosure of the secret program has been kept from the public and Congress.

It is also claimed the Senate Select Committee on Intelligence were also unaware of the secret program until the PCLOB report was delivered, March 2021.

The Senators close the letter by reiterating the importance of declassiciation of the "Deep Dive II" report.

They also call for the declassification of two other reports related to Executive Order 12333.
